Makes 1 cup 

- 1/4 cup chopped walnuts (toasting optional) 
- 1/4 cup walnut vinegar or white wine vinegar 
- 1 tablespoon grainy mustard 
- 1/2 teaspoon salt 
- Freshly ground black pepper to taste 
- 1/3 cup walnut oil 
- 1/3 cup pure olive oil 
- 1 small clove garlic, peeled and chopped 
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh tarragon 
- 2 tablespoons chopped parsley 

1. If toasting the walnuts, put into a small, dry skillet and place over medium heat, shaking the pan occasionally, for 5 minutes. Cool. 

2. To prepare the vinaigrette by hand: Whisk together the vinegar, mustard, salt and pepper until slightly thickened. Combine the walnut and olive oils. Slowly add to the vinegar mixture, whisking until blended and emulsified. Stir in walnuts, finely chopped garlic, tarragon and parsley. 

To prepare the vinaigrette by food processor/blender: Put garlic, tarragon and parsley into the processor/blender and chop finely. Add the vinegar, mustard, salt and pepper; process to blend. With the motor running, slowly add the oils, processing until blended and emulsified. 

3. Pour the vinaigrette into a jar. Stir in walnuts, cover tightly and refrigerate up to 3 days. 

Serving notes: Serve with chicken, chicken salads, salads that include pears, apples or blue cheese.
