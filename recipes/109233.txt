Recipe: Basque Chicken 
4 to 6 servings

- 2 chorizo-flavored sausage links 
- 4 bone-in chicken thighs, skin removed 
- 3 tablespoons olive oil 
- 1 large onion, peeled and finely chopped 
- 2 to 4 medium cloves garlic, peeled and minced 
- 1 cup long-grain white rice
- « to ? teaspoon salt 
- ¬ teaspoon paprika 
- ¬ teaspoon fresh ground black pepper 
- Pinch cayenne pepper 
- 2« cups reduced-sodium and fat chicken broth, divided 
- 1 cup coarsely chopped roasted red bell pepper 
- 1 cup frozen peas 
- 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves


1. Put the sausage into a saucepan and cover with cold water. Bring to a boil, reduce heat and simmer 12 minutes. Drain. When cool enough to handle, slice into ¬-inch thick slices and set aside. 

2. In 5- to 6-quart pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Put chicken into pan and brown about 15 minutes or until partially cooked through. Add sausage and brown 2 minutes. Remove both from pot. 

3. Reduce heat to medium-low and put onion into pan. Cook 8 minutes, stirring often. Add garlic and continue cooking 2 minutes. Stir in rice, salt, paprika, pepper and cayenne. Stir about 2 minutes, until the rice is coated with the onion mixture. Put chicken and sausage back into pan. Pour in 2 cups broth and bring to a boil, stirring well. Cover, reduce heat to low and cook 15 minutes. 

4. Stir remaining « cup broth, bell pepper and peas into rice. Cover and cook 8 minutes. Test chicken for doneness; continue cooking if necessary. Remove pot from heat. Remove chicken from rice and stir in thyme leaves. Cover pot and set aside. Cut chicken from the bone and stir back into rice. Let sit 5 minutes before serving. 

Adapted from "A Flash in the Pan: Fast, Fabulous Recipes in a Single Skillet" by Brooke Dojny and Melanie Barnard
