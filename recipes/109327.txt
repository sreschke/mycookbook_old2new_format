1 cup plus 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
1/2 C pine nuts
2 and 1/2 cups tightly packed basil leaves (2 large bunches)
2 garlic cloves, peeled
2 tablespoons plus 1/2 teaspoon salt
1/3 cup freshly grated parmesean cheese
1 cup freshly grated romano
1 pound linguini

heat 6-inch skillit over medium flame. add 2 tablespoons of the oilive oil and the pine nuts and cook five minutes, or until the pine nuts are golden, stirring often.

proccess the toasted pinutes to a paste in a food processor with the basil, garlic and 1/2 teaspoon of the salt. gradully pour in the remaining cup of olive oil with the motor running; the mixture will emulsify.

transfer the pesto to a serving bowl and stir in the cheeses with a fork. (the pesto keeps in the regrigerator for up to one week, as long as it's topped with a thin layer of olive oil; it can also be frozen for up to one month w/o the cheese stirred in.)

when you're ready to serve, bring 5 quarts of water to a boil.  add the linguini and the remaining 2 tablespoons of salt and cook al dente; drain, reserving 1/2 cup of pasta cooking water.

fold 1/4 cup of the reserved water into the pesto in the serving bowl. add the drained linguini and stir to coate. dilute with additional water if needed. adjust the salt if neccicary and serve hot.
