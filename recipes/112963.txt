3 slices high quality sandwich bread, crusts removed
4 oz. plain high quality potato chips (Terra Yukon Gold are good)
6 T. chopped fresh dill
1 whole side of salmon fillet, about 3 1/2 lbs
1 t. olive oil
3/4 t. salt
ground black pepper
3 T. dijon mustard

1. Adjust one oven rack to uppermost position and second rack to upper middle position; heat oven to 400 degrees.
2. Pulse bread in workbowl of food processor fitted with steel blade until processed into faairly even 1/4 inch pieces about the size of Grap Nuts cereal about ten second pulses. Spread crumbs evenly on rimmed baking sheet; toast on lower rack, shaking pan once or twice, until golden brown and crisp, 4-5 minutes. Toss together with potato chips and dill; set aside.
3. Increase oven setting to broil. Cut piece of  heavy duty foil 6" longer than fillet. Fold foil lenth-wise in thirds and place lengthwise on rimmed baking sheet; position salmon lengthwise on foil, allowing excess foil to overhang baking sheet. Rub fillet evenly with oil; sprinkle with salt and pepper. Broil salmon on upper rack until surface is spotty brown and outer 1/2 inch of thick end is opaque when gently flaked with paring knife, 9 to 11 minutes. Remove fish from oven, spread evenly with mustard, and press bread crumb mixture onto fish. Return to lower rack and continue broiling until crust is deep golden brown, about 1 minute.
SERVES 8 TO 10

VARIATION: WITH CHUTNEY
Heat 2 T. unsalted butter in small skillet over medium heat until melted. Off heat, add 1 medium garlic clove, minced; 1/2 t. each ground cumin and paprikea; and 1/4 t. each ground cinnamon, cayenne and salt. Set aside. Follow recipe for Broiled Salmon with Mustard...substituting 3 T. chopped fresh parsley for dill, tossing butter-spice mixture into breadcrumbs/chips and using smooth mango chutney instead of Dijon mustard.
