2 cups walnuts, finely chopped
1 cup walnuts, finely chopped (optional this is found in greek baklava)
1 cup sugar
2 cups clarified, melted butter 
2 tsp. cinnamon
1 tbsp. orange blossom water (mahazar) 
1 package phyllo dough (1 lb. or 454 gms.)

mix the walnuts (almonds if using), sugar, 1/4 cup
of butter, cinnamon & orange blossom water, then set aside.

butter well a 13x9 inch baking pan, then set aside.

taking one sheet of the phyllo dough & place it in the baking dish, then brush w/ melted butter. repeat this process until 1/2 of the dough is used. then place the walnut mixture of the buttered layers
& spread evenly.

place one layer of dough on the nut mixture & brush w/ melted butter, then continue this until the rest of the dough is used.

heat the remaining butter, then pour it evenly over the dough. w/ a sharp knife, carefully cut the pastry into 2 inch sq or diamond shapes. bake in a preheated oven of 400 f, then lower the heat to 300 f. & bake for 30 to 45 mins or until the sides are a light shade of brown.

while the baklawa is baking, prepare the syrup & set aside.

2 syrup variations (choose one for your pastry delight!)

egyptian sugar syrup or sharbat
2 cups sugar
1 cup water
2 tbsps lemon juice
2 tbsps orange blossom water (optional)

place the sugar & water in a pot over med heat.

stir constantly for 10 mins or until the sugar is throughly dissolved.

remove from the heat, then stir in the lemon juice. return to the heat & bring it to a boil. remove again & stir in the orange blossom water (if using),
then allow the syrup to cool until the pastry is ready.
