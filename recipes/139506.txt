FOR THE VINDALOO PASTE:
2 fresh jalapenos (use half the seeds for heat)
1/8 cup vinegar
1 tablespoon cumin seeds
2/3 tablespoon black mustard seeds
1/3 tablespoon tumeric
1/3 tablespoon black pepper
1 dash ground cinnamon
1 dash cardamon seeds
1 dash ground cloves
1 dash ground nutmeg
1/3 large onion, chopped
4 cloves garlic, peeled
1/3 inch piece fresh ginger, coarsely chopped
3/8 cup olive oil
1/4 cup cider vinegar
1/3 tablespoon sugar
1 dash salt

TO FINISH:
1/2 cup chopped cilantro

FOR THE BEANS:
1 pound dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
1 medium onion, chopped
1 good lug olive oil
2 big boxes low-salt chicken stock
all of the above vindaloo paste

Take a couple days to make this.  First day, make the paste and soak the beans.  

To make the paste: Soak the chilies in vinegar for 20 minutes.  Add chopped onion and garlic cloves to a blender.  Roast the spices in a skillet for about 1 minute, then grind in spice grinder.  Add to the blender.  Add remaining paste ingredients to blender.  Whiz into a paste.  Store covered in frig.  

Second day, make the dish.  Fry the onions in the oil til soft and beginning to brown.  Then, add all the paste; continue frying a little.  Add a little stock.  Stir to blend.  Then add the beans and rest of the stock.  Cover and cook on medium for over 1 hour, til chickpeas are very tender.  While beans are cooking, chop the cilantro.  Serve with chopped cilantro over top.
