The flavors in this autumn squash soup are very subtle-the apple adds sweetness and the curry powder provides a touch of spice. 

2 tablespoons butter or margarine
1 medium onion, chopped
1 carrot, peeled and chopped
1 teaspoon curry powder
3 cans (13.75 or 14.5 oz. each)chicken broth or 5.25 cups Rich Chicken Stock 
2 large butternut squash (about 3 lbs.), peeled, seeded, and coarsely chopped
2 baking apples, peeled, cored, and coarsely chopped
1/4 teaspoon freshly ground pepper
Plain low-fat yogurt and apple slices, for garnish

1. Melt butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add onion, carrot, and curry powder; cook until onion is softened, 5 minutes. Add chicken broth, squash, apples, and pepper. Simmer until vegetables are tender, 12 minutes. 
2. Puree soup in a blender in small batches and return each batch tot he saucepan. (Can be made ahead. Transfer soup to a large bowl and cool to room temperature. Cover and rifrigerate up to 2 days. Reheat in a saucepan 15 minutes.) Spoon soup into warm bowls and garnish each with a dollop of yogurt and apple slcies. Makes 10 servings.
