1/2 lb. dry red beans
1 lb. top sirloin
1 lb. chili-grind beef
2 lg. Spanish onions, diced
3 pounds freesh tomatoes, cubed
1 (16 oz) can tomato puree
5 cloves garlic, minced
1 (12-oz.) bottle stale Mexican beer (pour out and let it breathe awhile to get stale)
2 tsp. ground cumin
2 tsp. ground coriander
2 tsp. Mexican oregano
3 tsp. chili powder
3 bay leaves
salt to taste
white pepper

Rinse and cook beans in water until tender, according to package directions.  Set aside.

Trim fat from sirloin.  Cut into 1/2 inch cubes and saute in dry pan until browned.  Remove cubes and add ground beef.  Saute until brown and crumbly.  Drain off excess fat.  Return sirloin cubes to pan and add onions, tomatoes, tomato puree, garlic, and beer.  Add cumin, coriander, oregano, chili powder, and bay leaves.  Season to taste with salt and pepper.  

Bring to boil.  Reduce heat, cover and simmer 1 1/2 to 2 hours.  Add beans to chili mixture and simmer until reheated, or serve beans on side.  Discard bay leaves and adjust seasoning to taste.  Makes 12 servings.

Each serving contains about 293 calories, 95 mg. sodium, 54 mg. cholesterol, 14 grams fat, 21 grams carbohydrates, 20 grams protein, 2 grams fiber, 43% calories from fat.
