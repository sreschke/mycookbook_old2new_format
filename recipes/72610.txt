Schnitz Und Knepp 

3 pounds ham
1 quart dried apples (schnitz)
2 cups flour
3 Tablespoons melted butter
2 Tablespoons brown sugar
4 teaspoons baking powder
1 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon pepper
1 egg, well-beaten
Milk

Wash dried apples and then cover with water to soak overnight. Cover ham with boiling water. Boil the ham for three hours. Add the soaked apples and the water that the apples were soaked in. Boil the ham and apples for 1 hour more. Add the sugar. Make dumplings (knepp) by sifting flour, salt, pepper, and baking powder together Stir in beaten egg, milk (enough to make fairly stiff, moist batter) and the butter. Drop batter by Tablespoonful into hot ham and apples. Cover and cook for 15 minutes for 15 minutes. Serve hot.   

From: Amish Cooking
Compiled by a committee of Amish women
Edited by: David Luthy 
ISBN: 0-517-19458-9
Alot of the recipes in the cookbook has been gathered from the Amish monthly magazine, "Family Life".
