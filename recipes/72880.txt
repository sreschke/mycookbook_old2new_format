3/4 cup fine dry bread crumbs
2Tbsp finely chopped yellow onion
1 tsp salt
1/8 tsp ground ginger
1/2 c. milk
1/2 lb lean ground beef
1/2 lb ground pork
1/2 lb ground veal
3 Tbsp butter
4 tsp all-purpose flour
1 c. beef broth
3/4 c. half-and-half

1.  In a large bowl, combine the bread crumbs, onion,salt, nutmeg, and milk.  Add the ground beef, pork, and veal, and mix thoroughly.  Shape the mixture into 1 1/2-inch balls. 

2.  Heat the butter in a heavy 12-inch skillet over moderate heat until bubbly.  Cook the meatballs, uncovered, turning frequently, for 20 min.  Remove them to a platter with a slotteed spoon.

3.  Add the flour to the drippings in the skillet, stir until bubbly, then blend in the beef broth and half-and-half.  Boil, uncovered, for 4 to 5 min, stirring, until the sauce thickens.  

4.  Return the meatballs and any accumulated juices to the skillet.  Spoon the sauce over the meatballs and heat through-about 2 minutes.

Tip: You can make this recipe using all ground beef.  Preparation: 13 minutes  Cooking: 27 minutes
