When your preferred fruit is out of season, try frozen, unsweetened peaches, blueberries, or raspberries--

2 c. raspberries or 2 c. chopped, peeled apples, apricots, peaches, or pineapple
1 T. lemon juice
1/2 c. sugar
3 T. cornstarch
1 1/2 c. all-purpose flour
1/2 c. sugar
1 1/2 t. baking powder
1/2 t. ground cinnamon
1/8 t. ground mace
1/2 c. margarine or butter
1 slightly beaten egg
1/2 c. milk
1/2 t. vanilla
1/4 c. sugar
1/4 c. all-purpose flour
2 T. margarine or butter
1/4 c. chopped walnuts

In a medium saucepan combine fruit and 1/2 c. water. Simmer, covered, for 5 minutes or till fruit is tender. Stir in lemon juice. Mix the 1/2 c. sugar and cornstarch; stir into fruit mixture. Cook and stir till thickened. Cool.

In a mixing bowl stir together 1 1/2 c. flour, 1/2 c. sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, mace, and 1/4 t. salt. Cut in 1/2 c. margarine or butter till mixture resembles fine crumbs. Combine egg, milk, and vanilla. Add to flour mixture, mixing till blended. Spread half of the batter in a greased 9x9x2 baking pan.

Spread the cooled fruit over batter. Spread remaining batter over fruit. Combine the 1/4 c. sugar and 1/4 c. flour; cut in the 2 T. butter till mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in nuts. Sprinkle nut mixture over batter in pan. Bake in 350 oven for 40 to 45 min. or till done. Cool. Makes 12 servings.

Better Homes and Gardens, Nov. 1987
